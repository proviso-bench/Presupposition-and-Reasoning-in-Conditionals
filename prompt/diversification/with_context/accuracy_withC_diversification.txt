TASK OVERVIEW

You will be provided with:
1. Information about the benchmark to be evaluated,
2. The main concept being assessed in the benchmark, and
3. Seed questions related to this concept.

Your task is to generate additional yes/no sub-questions that expand and diversify the seed questions in order to comprehensively assess the main concept.

Each generated question must meet the conditions and constraints below to ensure a high-quality evaluation checklist.

---

1. BENCHMARK INFORMATION

This benchmark evaluates how language models reason about conditional statements of the form “If A, B,” where B contains a presupposition trigger (e.g., his, her, their).
Models are also provided with a brief identifying sentence about the person mentioned in the conditional and target statement, which is intended solely for identification and not for inference.
Models are also given a non-conditional target statement that corresponds to the presupposition of B.
The 0–7 score reflects the model’s judgment of how likely this presupposition is to be true.

Models are asked to provide step-by-step reasoning and a numerical likelihood rating (0–7) for each target statement.
The goal of the benchmark is to analyze the quality of the model’s reasoning process and its handling of presupposition in conditional sentences, rather than surface-level correctness.

---

2. MAIN CONCEPT IN THE BENCHMARK

Concept: Logical Accuracy

Description:
Logical Accuracy refers to whether the model correctly implements the logical semantics of conditionals and avoids formal reasoning fallacies, such as treating conditionals as biconditionals.

---

3. SEED QUESTIONS

- Does the model correctly treat the sentence as a conditional?
- Does the model avoid treating the conditional as evidence that the antecedent is true?

---

CONDITIONS FOR A GOOD QUESTION LIST

- Each question must be answerable with “Yes” or “No”.
- A “Yes” answer must indicate better logical reasoning quality.
- Each question must assess only one logical phenomenon.
- Each question must correspond to an observable reasoning behavior.
- Each question must be grounded in formal conditional logic.

---

CONSTRAINTS

- Do not combine multiple concepts in a single question.
- Do not introduce new evaluation dimensions.
- Do not ask about stylistic, grammatical, or writing quality.
- Do not generate vague, subjective, or impressionistic questions.
- Focus exclusively on logical interpretation of conditional statements.
